Research Work

Master's thesis research on remote photoplethysmography (rPPG) for contactless heart rate estimation — conducted at CMVS, University of Oulu, under Professor Guoying Zhao.

Remote Photoplethysmography for Contactless Heart Rate Estimation

My thesis research focuses on estimating heart rate from facial videos without any contact sensors — using subtle color changes in skin caused by blood flow (the PPG signal). This enables health monitoring applications in consumer electronics, clinical settings, and wearables like the Oura Ring.

I implement and evaluate both traditional handcrafted signal processing algorithms and modern deep learning models, comparing their accuracy, robustness, and generalizability under real-world conditions including varying lighting, head motion, skin tones, and recording distances.

Methodology

Methods Implemented & Evaluated

Deep Learning
PhysNet
3D convolutional network for spatiotemporal rPPG feature extraction from raw video segments.
Deep Learning
PhysFormer
Transformer-based architecture for long-range temporal dependency modeling in rPPG signals.
Handcrafted
CHROM
Chrominance-based method exploiting skin color's unique chrominance signature for robust signal extraction.
Handcrafted
POS
Plane-Orthogonal-to-Skin method — projects RGB signals onto a plane orthogonal to the skin color subspace.
Handcrafted
GREEN
Green channel signal extraction — baseline method exploiting the dominant PPG component in green wavelengths.
Ensemble
SQA_VOTE
Signal Quality Assessment voting ensemble combining multiple methods — achieves 4.54 BPM MAE on OBF dataset.

  • Skin tone diversity across subjects
  • Short and long video durations
  • Side-face and frontal conditions
  • Metrics: MAE, RMSE, Pearson correlation
