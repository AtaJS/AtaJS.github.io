OAI Dataset Analysis: Data Processing, Visualization & Hypothesis Testing

Context: Data Analysis · Clinical Dataset · University of Oulu
Clinical Data Statistics Data Mining

A comprehensive statistical analysis of the Osteoarthritis Initiative (OAI) dataset — a large-scale, multi-center human research study involving thousands of participants with or at risk of knee osteoarthritis (OA). The project covered the full data analysis pipeline from raw data ingestion to statistical inference.

Data Processing

The OAI dataset contains hundreds of clinical and demographic variables. Preprocessing involved handling missing values, outlier detection, data type normalization, and feature selection. Particular attention was paid to preserving the clinical meaning of variables such as Kellgren-Lawrence (KL) grades, which encode radiographic OA severity on a 0–4 scale.

Visualization

Distribution plots, correlation matrices, and group comparison charts were produced to explore OA prevalence across demographics. KL grade distributions were visualized by age group, BMI category, and sex to identify clinically relevant patterns in the dataset.

Hypothesis Testing

Two-sample hypothesis tests (t-tests, Mann-Whitney U, chi-square) were applied to investigate statistically significant differences between participant subgroups. Results were evaluated at α = 0.05 with appropriate corrections for multiple comparisons.

Stack: Python · NumPy · Pandas · SciPy · Matplotlib · Seaborn
