Geometry-Aware Attention for Remote Photoplethysmography Under Head Rotation
Hui Wei, Ata Jodeiri Seyedian, Guoying Zhao
28th ACM International Conference on Multimodal Interaction (ICMI 2026) — Expected October 2026
Remote photoplethysmography (rPPG) methods using deep learning have achieved strong performance under controlled conditions, but accuracy degrades significantly when subjects rotate their heads, as facial surface orientation toward the camera changes. This work proposes a geometry-aware attention mechanism that integrates 3D facial surface orientation derived from MediaPipe landmarks into the FactorizePhys attention module. By modulating spatial attention weights based on surface geometry, the method improves heart rate estimation robustness under head rotation while maintaining computational efficiency. Experiments on MMPD, VIPL-HR, PURE, and UBFC-rPPG datasets demonstrate improved performance under rotation conditions compared to geometry-blind baselines.
